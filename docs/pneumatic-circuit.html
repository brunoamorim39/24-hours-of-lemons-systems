Pneumatic Notes

  • Both valve exhaust ports (EA and EB) vent to atmosphere. Keep them unobstructed and pointed somewhere the brief vent-puff won't spray debris onto anything sensitive. Mufflers/silencers thread onto G1/4 if cabin noise is a concern.
  • Fail-safe behavior (flap closes) is triggered by any of: ESP32 goes low on GPIO25, Controller Rocker OFF, Air Rocker OFF (kills coil and compressor cycling), brake interlock, or 12V bus failure. All of those de-energize the coil; the spool spring shifts; tank air routes through port B to the rod end and retracts the piston. The only failure that leaves the flap stuck is simultaneous coil de-energize + completely empty tank.
  • Unlike the single-acting variant we prototyped earlier, there is no cylinder-side spring and no QEV. Retract speed is governed by regulator pressure, valve Cv, tubing ID, and the size of exhaust port EA. If retract is too slow, raise regulator pressure (within cylinder spec) before touching anything else.
  • Cylinder mounting: watch rod alignment to the flap linkage. Side-loading the rod causes premature seal wear on the MAL series. A clevis or floating mount on both ends is the forgiving choice.
  • Shutdown: Air Rocker OFF → Controller Rocker OFF → engine off → open the Viair tank drain petcock (underside of tank) to bleed residual pressure. Close the petcock before next use.
  • Regulator setting: start low (40–50 PSI), verify the flap extends fully and crisply, verify retract is clean and fast, then tune up only if needed. Running higher than required wastes air, stresses fittings, and makes the valve click louder on every actuation.
